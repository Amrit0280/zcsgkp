Section 01
General Rules
15 Rules
  • 1Every child must come to school on time. Late arrivals may not be admitted to class without the Principal's permission.
  • 2Perfect silence should be maintained during the prayer bell.
  • 3Students need to bring the school diary daily.
  • 4Students are required to note their homework in the school diary.
  • 5Students are to keep their surroundings clean and use the dustbins provided.
  • 6Boys are to keep their nails and hair short.
  • 7Girls are to keep their nails short and braid their long hair.
  • 8Students are advised not to buy food from street vendors in the interest of their health.
  • 9Students are strictly forbidden to leave the school premises or grounds during regular school hours. Permission to leave may be granted only by the Principal.
  • 10Students must park their cycles/scooters in the parking area and lock them. Students riding two-wheelers must wear helmets and possess a driving licence.
  • 11Lending or borrowing of money is strictly prohibited. The school authorities will not be responsible for loss of money, books, etc. Students are expected to look after their own belongings.
  • 12Students caught triple riding will be penalized.
  • 13Physical training, games, tours, picnics and camps are compulsory for all students. Students are required to take part in various extracurricular activities organized from time to time.
Section 02
Discipline Rules
3 Rules
  • 1No student will manhandle another student. It is considered a serious offence. Any violation of this rule may result in expulsion of the offender from the school. Students are expected to exhibit exemplary behaviour inside and outside the school premises, worthy of children brought up in decent homes.
  • 2The Principal is empowered to rusticate, suspend, or remove a student from the roll of the school on grounds of indiscipline, misbehaviour, neglect of academic work, or frequent absence.
  • 3Students must refrain from damaging school property — furniture, fans, window panes, etc. Any knowledge of accidental or intentional damage must be reported to the Head Mistress. Damage will be remedied at the cost of the offender.
Section 03
Attendance & Leave
2 Rules
  • 1No leave of absence is granted except on a previous application from parent/guardian for plausible reasons only, or on an application from the parent when the student reports back to school.
  • 2Absence without leave or unexplained absence for more than three consecutive days may result in the student's name being struck off the rolls. Re-admission may only be granted on payment of a fresh admission fee.
Section 04
Uniform Rules
2 Rules
  • 1A student who is not dressed neatly and tidily in full school uniform will not be permitted to sit in class. The prescribed shade and pattern must be carefully adhered to. Any deviation will not be accepted. Strict measures will be taken against those who ignore the uniform rule.
  • 2Students must wear house dress on Wednesday and Saturday.
Section 05
Guidelines for Parents
8 Points
Check your ward's school diary daily and ensure all work is done.
Sign the progress report of your ward after each examination.
Maintain and sign leave records in the school diary; send advance applications to school.
Ensure regularity, punctuality, discipline and completion of homework. Encourage active participation in all school activities.
Keep children at home during infectious illness. A medical fitness certificate is required before the child returns to school.
Notify the school in writing of any change in address or mobile number.
Ensure all fees and dues are cleared on time without exception.
Adhere to visiting hours. No teacher is to be contacted during school hours without the prior permission of the Principal/Administrator.
Section 06
Important Notice
Mandatory
Strict regularity and punctuality, implicit obedience, courtesy in speech and conduct, cleanliness of person and uniform are expected from all students. Parents are expected to co-operate with school authorities regarding these instructions, rules and regulations.

A student may be removed by school authorities without prior notice to the parents on any of the following grounds:
Constant weakness in studies
Habitual indiscipline & disobedience
Irregular attendance
Behavioural problems / involvement in violence
Indifference to school activities
Untidy or improper uniform
Non-cooperation of parents / guardians with school staff
